# 30-Minute Daily Playbook

The full sales kit is now ~21 documents — overwhelming if you try to do
all of it. This is the minimal daily routine that, done consistently for
60-90 days, produces compounding inbound.

**Total time per day: 30 minutes.** Pick one block per day from the
weekly rotation. Don't try to do all of them in one session.

---

## Weekly rotation

**Monday — LinkedIn outbound (30 min)**
- 10 connection requests (use `21-linkedin-outbound-playbook.md`,
  templates A/B/C)
- 5 first DMs to people who accepted last week
- 3 bumps to people who didn't reply 7 days ago
- 1 value drop to people who didn't reply 14 days ago
- Update your tracking sheet

**Tuesday — Reddit / Quora answer drop (30 min)**
- Pick 1 question from `18-reddit-quora-answers.md`
- Find 2-3 active threads asking that question (Reddit + Quora search)
- Post one helpful answer (not pasted — adapted to the specific thread)
- Reply to anyone who responds within 24 hours

**Wednesday — Email follow-ups (30 min)**
- Review yesterday's leads (web form submits)
- Send personal first-touch email (not the auto-nurture — manual reply)
  to anyone whose application looks worth pursuing
- Triage replies to existing nurture sequences (any "questions" or
  "objections")

**Thursday — Content / asset creation (30 min)**
- Pick one piece: blog post, YouTube short, IG post, LinkedIn post
- 30 minutes total: 15 to draft, 15 to publish + promote
- Minimum cadence: 1 piece per week. Compounds over time.

**Friday — Pipeline review (30 min)**
- Check this week's stats (connection accepts, DM replies, demos booked,
  closes)
- Identify what worked + what didn't (write 1-2 sentences in a journal)
- Adjust message templates if reply rates dropped

**Weekend — Skip.** Don't burn out. Outbound on weekends has worse reply
rates anyway.

---

## What 30 min/day produces (90-day forecast)

If you do 30 min/day, 5 days/week, for 90 days:

**Volume**:
- ~150 LinkedIn connection requests sent
- ~75 first DMs sent
- ~40 Reddit/Quora answers posted
- ~20 web form lead emails sent
- ~12 pieces of original content created

**Conversion outcomes** (conservative):
- 50 LinkedIn replies → 12-15 demos booked → 3-5 SaaS closes
- Reddit answers compound: ~500 organic visits, ~3-5 paying customers
- 12 content pieces drive ~100-300 organic visits, ~2-5 customers
- Email follow-ups on warm leads: ~5-10 conversions

**Revenue forecast** (90 days):
- ~3-5 SaaS closes × $400 avg MRR × 12mo LTV = $14k-$24k LTV
- ~5-10 mailbox plans × $40 avg MRR × 18mo LTV = $3k-$7k LTV
- ~1-2 $2k bundle closes = $2k-$4k
- Total: $19k-$35k LTV in pipeline by day 90

That's the floor at minimum effort. Doubling time invested doesn't
double output — but going from 0 to 30 min/day is the biggest delta.
The next biggest delta is consistency. **Don't skip days.**

---

## What NOT to do

- **Don't try to do all the sales kit at once.** It's a 21-doc library.
  Pull the parts you need.
- **Don't automate LinkedIn** (auto-DM tools = ban risk).
- **Don't paste Reddit answers verbatim.** Adapt every one to the thread.
- **Don't ghost replies.** If someone replies to your DM, respond within
  24 hours.
- **Don't pitch in connection requests.** It's the #1 thing that kills
  acceptance rates.

---

## Stop conditions

If after 60 days you're seeing:
- LinkedIn acceptance rate below 25% → profile or message problem
- DM reply rate below 10% → message problem
- Demo booking rate below 30% on replies → profile credibility problem

Diagnose before increasing volume. Volume amplifies the problem.

If you're seeing:
- Acceptance rate 30-40% + DM reply rate 15-25% + demo booking 30-40%
  → double volume

## Tracking template (Google Sheets)

Columns:
- Date
- Channel (LinkedIn / Reddit / Email / Content)
- Action (sent X DMs / posted X answers / etc.)
- Outcome (replies / clicks / signups credited)
- Notes

One row per day. Five minutes at end of day. After 30 days you'll see
the patterns.

---

## When to scale

Scale up when:
1. Your tracking shows consistent 30 min/day for 60 days
2. Your conversion math is positive (LTV > effort)
3. You're hitting the ceiling of what 30 min produces

How to scale:
- Hire a virtual assistant (LinkedIn outreach is delegate-able once
  templates are stable)
- Pay an SDR for outbound calling (not in our current playbook — but
  available)
- Increase content cadence from 1/week to 2-3/week
- Run paid ads against your best-converting organic content (use
  `19-paid-search-keyword-pack.md`)

## What's next after 90 days

If 90 days of consistent execution produces ~$20-35k LTV in pipeline,
the next 90 days should compound:
- Reddit answers stay live forever — keep generating organic
- Content compounds in search rankings (3-6 months to peak)
- LinkedIn connections become warm relationships you can DM later

Year 1 target: $200k-400k pipeline LTV at 30 min/day. Year 2: 2-3x as
content matures.

For the $5M net monthly goal, the 30-min/day playbook alone won't get
us there. But it's the foundation — the deeper plan is in
`16-paths-to-5m-net-monthly.md`. This playbook is what you do every day
to compound toward it.
