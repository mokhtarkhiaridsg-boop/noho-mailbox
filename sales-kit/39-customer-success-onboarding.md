# Customer Success Onboarding Playbook

The first 30 days are when customers either form a habit (they stay
2+ years) or fall off (they cancel within 90 days). This playbook
covers the exact onboarding sequence by product line.

**Principle**: A customer who isn&apos;t actively using your product
in week 1 is a churn risk in month 3. Onboarding is not "send the
welcome email" — it&apos;s actively shepherding them to first value.

---

## Universal first-value milestones

For every customer, the goal is to hit "first value" within 7 days:

| Product | First value moment |
|---|---|
| Mailbox plan | First piece of mail received and scanned |
| $2k Bundle | LLC + EIN delivered |
| Same-day delivery | First successful run |
| CMRA SaaS | First customer migrated to new platform |
| Affiliate program | First click on tracking link |
| Partner program | First referred client |

If a customer hasn&apos;t hit first value by day 7, intervene
manually. Send a personal email or call. Find out what&apos;s blocking
them.

## Mailbox Plan Onboarding (the most common signup)

### Day 0 (signup)

**Auto-actions** (system handles):
- Welcome email with box number, dashboard URL, Form 1583 link
- Calendar reminder set for day 7 to check Form 1583 status

**Manual actions** (founder / CS rep):
- Personal welcome email sent within 4 hours: "saw you signed up,
  walk-in is fastest if you&apos;re local — come by anytime Mon-Sat"
- Add to onboarding-tracking spreadsheet

### Day 1

**Auto-actions**: Day 1 check-in email (template from doc 20 Sequence
D email D2)

**Manual check**: Did they sign up via the dashboard? If they didn&apos;t
log in within 24 hours, something&apos;s wrong with the welcome email
or the dashboard URL.

### Day 3

**Auto-actions**: None scheduled.

**Manual check**: Has the customer set up Form 1583 yet? If no:
- If they&apos;re local: text or call to invite walk-in for free
  notary
- If remote: send Form 1583 link + notary directions

### Day 7

**Auto-actions**: Day 7 check-in email (template from doc 20 D3) with
common questions list.

**Manual check**:
- Has Form 1583 been processed and filed?
- Has any mail been received and scanned?
- Has the customer logged in to the dashboard at least once?

If ANY of these are no: personal call. Mailbox customers who haven&apos;t
hit first value by day 7 churn at 3-4x the rate of customers who have.

### Day 14

**Auto-actions**: Onboarding-completion summary email — "you&apos;re
all set, here&apos;s what to expect in months 1-3."

**Manual check**: review their dashboard activity. Are they actively
using it? Cross-reference customer feedback / support tickets / any
issues raised.

### Day 30

**Auto-actions**: 30-day review-request email + soft upsell (template
D4 from doc 20).

**Manual actions**:
- Review their first month: any issues? Refund requests? Complaints?
- If they&apos;re a Business or Premium plan customer: send a
  personal "you&apos;ve been with us a month — anything we can do
  better?" email.
- Track which customers never logged in past day 1. Flag for special
  attention.

## $2k Business Launch Bundle Onboarding

This is the most-engaged customer experience because there&apos;s
real work to do. The 14-day timeline is fixed; missing milestones
hurts churn AND your refund rate.

### Day 0 (purchase)

**Manual actions** (founder runs this start-to-finish):
- Send intake form within 1 hour: "send me your business name
  preferences, what state you want to register in (just CA?), your
  brand vision, and your domain ideas"
- Put kickoff call on calendar for day 2 (60 minutes)
- Add to bundle-tracking spreadsheet with 14-day milestones

### Day 2 (kickoff call)

60-minute call covering:
- Business goals (where are you in 12 months?)
- Brand direction (3-5 visual references they&apos;ve seen and like)
- Website content (5 pages we&apos;ll build — what goes on each?)
- Domain selection (we&apos;ll register day 3)
- Operating Agreement specifics (multi-member? S-corp election?
  any unusual clauses?)
- Mail forwarding preferences
- Contact at the bank (we&apos;ll intro them day 12-14)

After the call: send a 1-page recap. Customer signs off on the
recap. That becomes the spec for the work.

### Day 3-5

**Brand work**: designer briefed. 3-5 logo options + color palette by
day 5.

**LLC filing**: filed on day 3. Receipt sent to customer.

**Domain**: registered day 3.

### Day 6-9

**Brand approval**: customer picks final logo by day 7.

**EIN**: applied day 6 (right after LLC is acknowledged by SOS).

**Website**: site builder briefed. Wireframes day 8, draft v1 day 9.

### Day 10-12

**Website iteration**: customer reviews v1, sends edits, we ship v2
by day 12.

**Operating Agreement**: drafted by day 11. Customer reviews +
signs.

**Mailbox**: address activated. Form 1583 notarized at our counter
(or via mobile notary if customer remote).

### Day 13-14 (delivery)

**Bank intro**: introduce customer to Mercury or Chase rep with
their LLC docs in hand.

**Final delivery**:
- LLC stamped
- EIN letter
- Operating Agreement signed
- Brand kit (logo, colors, typography in PDF + PNG + SVG)
- Website live at customer&apos;s domain
- Mailbox active with notarized Form 1583

**Send**: a wrap-up email with all credentials + login URLs +
"you&apos;re live, here&apos;s how to use it all."

### Day 21 (1-week post-delivery)

**Manual actions**:
- "How&apos;s the first week been?" email
- Address any post-delivery questions
- Soft pitch the affiliate program (some bundle customers become
  great affiliates)

### Day 30 (1-month post-delivery)

**Manual actions**:
- 30-day check-in email
- Review request (Google review)
- Case study request (if they&apos;ve had a quick win)

### Day 60-90

Mailbox plan onboarding from above continues from this point. Bundle
customers get the full mailbox onboarding sequence in addition.

## Same-Day Delivery Onboarding

For one-time runs, "onboarding" is just doing the run successfully.
For B2B regulars, real onboarding starts.

### B2B regular customer onboarding

Customer who books 5+ runs in their first month gets contacted for
a "let&apos;s set you up properly" call.

**Goals of the call**:
- Standardize pickup / dropoff locations
- Set up recurring routes (5-9 runs/week pricing tier)
- Identify a primary contact + backup
- Set up dispatch communication preferences (email / SMS / both)
- Confirm billing setup (auto-charge after each run vs weekly invoice)

**Day 30 check-in**: have we hit their expected volume? Any issues
with specific drivers / routes / timing?

## CMRA SaaS Onboarding (highest-value)

This is the most intensive onboarding because the customer is
running their entire business on the software.

### Week 1: Data import

- Customer list import (CSV)
- Stripe Connect setup
- Form 1583 archive import
- Storefront branding configured (logo, colors, custom domain)

### Week 2: Parallel running

- New customers go to the new platform
- Existing customers continue on old until billing cycle
- Staff trained on admin dashboard
- Daily check-in calls (15 min) with founder

### Week 3: Cutover

- Migrate existing customers (one-time email + Stripe migration)
- Update USPS quarterly reporting to new system
- Customer-facing announcement (template provided)
- Support availability stepped up to 2x daily check-ins for 1 week

### Week 4: Stabilization

- Reduce check-ins to weekly
- Address any customer complaints
- Identify any feature gaps / customizations needed
- Begin upsell conversations (add same-day delivery? add packing &
  shipping?)

### Day 90 review

Quarterly review with operator:
- Active customer count
- Revenue change (vs pre-migration baseline)
- Outstanding issues
- Feature roadmap requests
- Health check — are they likely to renew?

## Affiliate / Partner Onboarding

### Affiliate Day 0

- Welcome email with tracking code (doc 20 Sequence C email C1)
- Asset pack link (doc 27)
- "Reply with your channel + audience size + first promotion idea"

### Affiliate Day 7

- Asset pack 2 sent (specific to their channel type — YouTube vs

### Affiliate Day 30

- Performance check-in (clicks, signups, earnings credited)
- "What&apos;s working / what isn&apos;t" question

### Affiliate Day 90

- "Top affiliates do these 3 things" email
- Co-branded landing page offer (if they&apos;ve earned >$2k)

- Welcome packet
- Referral link
- "Reply with first 3 expected referrals so we can prep"

### Partner Day 30

- "How are referrals going?" check-in
- Review what worked / what didn&apos;t in their conversations

## Common churn signals (intervene before they cancel)

**Mailbox plan churn signals**:
- No login in 14+ days
- Mail volume dropping (they&apos;re not getting much mail)
- Support ticket about payment issue
- "I&apos;m moving" mentioned anywhere

**$2k Bundle churn signals**:
- Refund request in first 14 days = bundle didn&apos;t deliver
- LLC filed but no website / brand activity = scope wasn&apos;t aligned
- Customer ghosting after delivery = not what they expected

**SaaS churn signals**:
- Active customer count dropping for 2+ months
- Support tickets escalating in tone
- Long gap (3+ weeks) without contact
- Their referrals to us drop to zero

When a churn signal appears: pick up the phone. Email won&apos;t
save it.

## What to track

Per customer:
- Date of signup
- Plan / product
- Activation date (first value moment)
- Time to activation (in days)
- Last login date
- Last support ticket date
- NPS score (if collected)
- Renewal status (active / paused / canceled)
- Estimated LTV (cumulative spend)

Aggregate weekly:
- New customers this week
- Activations this week (% of new customers who hit first value)
- Churn this week
- Net new (new - churn)

## Customer success ownership

**Solo / small team**: founder owns CS personally. 30 min/week is
enough at <50 active customers.

**Once you have 50+ customers**: hire a CS person (sales kit doc 36
Stage 4 hire). Their first 30 days = read this playbook + shadow
existing customer interactions.

**At 200+ customers**: split CS into onboarding (first 30 days) and
ongoing relationship (30+ days). Different skill sets.

---

## Common mistakes

1. **Treating onboarding as automated emails only.** Auto-emails are
   step 1. Manual touch is what closes the activation.
2. **Waiting for problems to surface.** Proactive check-ins catch
   90% of problems before they become churn.
3. **Spending equal time on all customers.** High-value customers
   ($2k bundle, SaaS) deserve more touch than $50 mailbox plans.
4. **Onboarding stops at day 7.** Onboarding really lasts 30-90 days
   for high-value plans. Cut short = churn.
5. **No follow-up on activation failures.** If a customer doesn&apos;t
   activate by day 7, you HAVE to find out why.

---

## The big picture

Customer acquisition is expensive. Customer churn is more expensive.

A customer who churns in month 3 cost you to acquire (CAC) and
generated maybe 1.5x that in revenue before leaving. Net: roughly
break-even.

A customer who stays 24 months generates 8-12x CAC in revenue. Net:
healthy LTV/CAC ratio.

The difference between break-even and 8-12x is the first 30 days.
Onboarding investment is the highest-ROI work you can do.

Don&apos;t skip onboarding to save time. The math doesn&apos;t work.
