# International CMRA Operator Prospect Research

How to find CMRA / private-mailbox / virtual-office operators in
non-US markets to license our SaaS to. Country-by-country playbook.

**Why this matters**: Mailbox / delivery / $2k bundle stay US-only
(physical LA). CMRA SaaS scales globally. International is the
unlimited-leverage lane for $5M MRR target.

**Legal note**: We license software. Operators are responsible for
their own country&apos;s postal-regulatory compliance. We&apos;re a
SaaS vendor, not a regulated postal entity in their jurisdiction.

---

## Universal prospect-research framework

For any country, find operators by:

1. **Local Yellow Pages / business directories** for terms equivalent
   to "private mailbox," "virtual office," "mailbox rental"
2. **Google Maps** for the local-language CMRA term (see country
   sections below)
3. **LinkedIn Sales Navigator** with location + industry filter:
   "Postal Services" + "Real Estate Services" + small-business
   filters
4. **National post directories** (Royal Mail PLB list in UK, etc.)
5. **Industry associations** (most have member directories)

Filter for ICP fit:
- 50+ active mailboxes (signal: visible address suite numbers, ad
  copy mentioning capacity, multiple staff)
- 1-10 employees (LinkedIn)
- 3+ years operating (less likely to fail mid-engagement)
- Active website / SEO signals (operators investing in growth)
- NOT national chains (lock-in to internal IT)

## Time-zone strategy

- UK / EU: send in YOUR morning (PT). They get it midday-evening
  their time. Best-replied window is afternoon UK time = morning
  US Pacific.
- Canada: same time zones as US.
- Australia / NZ: send YOUR night for their morning. Plan demos
  around 2pm-5pm PT (which is 9am-12pm Sydney).
- Mexico: same time zones as US (mostly).

## What this playbook ISN&apos;T

- Not a substitute for an actual sales rep. International outbound
  works only if YOU (or hired SDR) execute consistently.
- Not for non-English operators in v1 (until we have multi-lingual
  UI).
- Not for highly-regulated markets (Germany&apos;s Postdienst,
  Japan&apos;s Yu-Pack network) — those need local compliance
  expertise.

International expansion compounds slowly. Year 1: 5-15 international
operators. Year 2: 30-60. Year 3: enough to justify hiring
international sales reps.

The compounding starts the first month. Don&apos;t skip it.
