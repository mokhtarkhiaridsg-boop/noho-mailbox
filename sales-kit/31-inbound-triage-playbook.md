# Inbound Triage Playbook

When inquiries land via the website forms, email, phone, walk-in, or
LinkedIn DMs — this playbook is what to do in the first 4 hours.

**The unifying principle**: speed + relevance + clarity. First-touch
response time is the #1 predictor of conversion in our data
(<1h response = 35% close, 4h response = 15% close, 24h+ response =
3% close).

Goal: every qualified inbound gets a personalized response within 4
business hours. Not a templated form letter — a real human read +
reply.

---

1. **Website forms** (highest volume, easiest to triage)

   - `/partners` apply form → partner program applications
   - `/contact` → general inquiries
   - Newsletter signups → low-signal, but route into email nurture

2. **Email** (mixed volume)

   - Replies to outbound campaigns → highest priority

3. **Phone** ((818) 506-7744)
   - Answer live during business hours
   - After hours → voicemail with same-day callback

4. **Walk-in storefront**
   - Front-counter staff handles initial qualification
   - Hand off to founder for qualified $2k bundle / SaaS leads

5. **LinkedIn DMs**
   - Reply within 24 hours (LinkedIn&apos;s slower-pace norm)

## Triage matrix

For each inbound, assign a tier in <60 seconds:

| Tier | Signal | First-touch time | Owner |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A — Hot** | $2k bundle inquiry, SaaS demo request, partner inquiry | <1 hour | Founder personally |
| **B — Warm** | Mailbox plan inquiry, delivery quote, affiliate apply | <4 hours | Founder OR sales staff |
| **C — Cold** | Newsletter signup, general "tell me more" | <24 hours | Auto-nurture (email sequence) |
| **D — Spam** | Generic SEO link-building outreach, "I noticed your website..." | Never | Mark as spam |

## Tier D — Spam / non-relevant

Common patterns to identify:
- "I noticed you don&apos;t rank for [keyword]..." (SEO link-builders)
- "I have crypto investment opportunities..." (scams)
- "I&apos;d like to write a guest post on your blog..." (link-builders)
- "Hi! I&apos;m [name] from [country]. I want to buy your business..."

Action: mark as spam, don&apos;t reply. Don&apos;t engage. Replying
adds you to a list that gets re-targeted.

## Phone calls during business hours

Answer with: "NOHO Mailbox, this is [Your Name]."

(Not: "How can I help you?" — sounds tier-1-helpdesk. Use your name.)

For each call:

1. **Listen first** for 10-15 seconds. Don&apos;t pitch immediately.
2. **Ask qualifying question**: "What got you to call us today?"
3. **Triage to right tier** based on answer:
   - Tier A → schedule longer call or walk-in this week
   - Tier B → answer questions live, push to action (sign up / book)
   - Tier C → take info, send email follow-up

**Calls about pricing**:
- Don&apos;t quote pricing on the phone before understanding use case.
- "Quick question — what are you trying to do? Once I know, I can give
  you the right number."
- Most pricing-led calls become "actually I need [different thing]"
  conversations.

**Calls that are clearly walk-in directions**:
- 5062 Lankershim Blvd, North Hollywood CA 91601.
- We&apos;re between Magnolia and Otsego, on the west side of
  Lankershim.
- Parking: 4 spots in front, free street parking after 6pm.

## Walk-in handling (front counter)

**Sequence for new walk-in**:

1. **Greet**: "Hey, welcome to NOHO Mailbox — what brings you in?"
2. **Listen** for 30 seconds. Don&apos;t pitch.
3. **Categorize**:
   - "I want a mailbox" → Tier B mailbox setup (15-min process)
   - "I&apos;m looking at addresses for my LLC" → Tier B with $2k
     bundle mention if appropriate
   - "I need to drop off a same-day delivery" → process the dispatch
   - "I&apos;m a CMRA operator looking at your software" → Tier A,
     hand off to founder
4. **Process** based on category. Walk-in mailbox setup is the
   fastest sale we have — average 12 minutes from "I want a mailbox"
   to "you have a mailbox."

**Walk-ins asking about prices in person**:
- Show them the printed pricing page (we keep one at the counter).
- Don&apos;t push the most expensive plan. Ask about use case first.
- Most walk-ins go to Basic ($50/3mo) or Business ($80/3mo). Premium
  is < 10% of walk-in conversions.

## CRM / Tracking

Whatever CRM you use (HubSpot, Pipedrive, Notion, even Google Sheets):

**Required fields per lead**:
- Date of inbound
- Channel (form / email / phone / walk-in / LinkedIn)
- Tier (A / B / C / D)
- Use case
- Contact info
- Status (new / replied / nurturing / closed-won / closed-lost)
- Owner
- Notes (free text)

**Weekly review**:
- New leads this week (by tier + channel)
- Response time (target: <1h for A, <4h for B, <24h for C)
- Conversion rate by tier
- Lost leads — why?

**Monthly review**:
- Top channel by close rate
- Top channel by volume
- LTV per channel (if calculable)
- Where to invest more time / budget next month

## Common mistakes

1. **Pitching before listening.** Always ask the qualifying question
   first. Pre-canned pitches feel inauthentic.
2. **Long replies to short questions.** Match their energy. If they
   sent 2 sentences, reply with 2-4 sentences. Not 10 paragraphs.
3. **Auto-replies that pretend to be human.** People can tell. Use
   honest auto-replies ("got your message — replying within 4 hours
   personally"), not fake-personal ones.
4. **Going dark.** If a Tier A lead doesn&apos;t reply, follow up.
   Once at 24h, again at 72h, then drop into auto-nurture. Don&apos;t
   ghost.
5. **Skipping the close question.** Every reply ends with a clear ask
   — "want to book?" / "ready to sign up?" / "next step?"

## Quick reference — response time targets

| Tier | First-touch | Follow-up #1 | Follow-up #2 |
|---|---|---|---|
| A | <1h | 24h if no reply | 72h |
| B | <4h | 48h | 5 days |
| C | <24h (auto) | per nurture seq | per nurture seq |
| D | never | — | — |

If you&apos;re consistently missing first-touch targets, you have too
much volume for one person. Either delegate or pause one of your
inbound channels until response capacity matches.

## When to escalate to founder

For any tier B or C lead that becomes:
- A SaaS demo request
- A partner program inquiry from a major firm
- A press / media request
- An investor / acquisition inquiry

Escalate immediately. These shape the business — they shouldn&apos;t
sit with a sales staffer.
