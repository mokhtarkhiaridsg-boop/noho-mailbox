# LinkedIn Outbound Playbook — CMRA SaaS

The CMRA SaaS engine is the highest-LTV lane in our 5-engine model
($299-$1,499/mo recurring, ~$3,500-$15,000 LTV per close). LinkedIn DMs
are the cleanest acquisition channel for it because the buyers (CMRA
owners, mailbox-business operators, USPS-CMRA-certified storefront
owners) are findable on LinkedIn with high signal-to-noise.

This playbook is built for one outbound seller working 1-2 hours/day,
sending 20-40 connection requests + 10-20 follow-up DMs per day. Target:
2-5 demo calls/week, 1-2 closes/month after the first 90 days.

---

## Profile setup (do this first)

Your LinkedIn profile is your landing page for every cold DM. Skip this
and your reply rate drops in half.

**Headline**: "Founder, NOHO Mailbox · CMRA SaaS for operators tired of giving up 30% to iPostal1"

(Or substitute "Anytime Mailbox" / "PostScan" if you find that target
network is more searched in your specific outbound batch.)

**Banner**: A clean photo of the storefront at 5062 Lankershim, with
"NOHO Mailbox · License the software · noho.la/cmra" text overlay.

**About section** (first 3 lines visible):
"I run NOHO Mailbox in Los Angeles. We process ~500 boxes a month and
handle same-day delivery for 50+ B2B accounts.

Two years ago we replaced iPostal1 with our own software because their
take rate was crushing margins. Now we license that software to other
CMRA operators tired of giving up 20-40% to a network."

**Featured section**: Pin
- Case study: nohomailbox.org/case-studies/noho-mailbox
- SaaS landing: nohomailbox.org/for-cmra-operators
- Migration playbook: nohomailbox.org/for-cmra-operators/migrate

**Recent activity**: Aim to post 1-2 times per week. CMRA-relevant content
(operational stats, case studies, screenshots of admin dashboard, customer
quotes). NOT inspirational quotes or hot takes.

## Target list — who to add

**Primary ICP** (highest fit):
- Owners / general managers / operations leads of:
  - Independent CMRA storefronts (search: "mailbox business," "private
    mailbox," "PMB owner," "postal store")
  - UPS Store franchisees who want to add CMRA-as-a-service
  - Pack-and-ship stores with idle counter space
  - Independent virtual mailbox operators on iPostal1 / Anytime / PostScan

**Secondary ICP** (good fit if primary is exhausted):
- Real estate investors who own commercial space and want to add a
  CMRA business as tenant revenue
- Postal-store franchise consultants
- Notary public franchisees considering adding CMRA

**Filter criteria for connection requests**:
- US-based (we license to US operators only for now)
- Active LinkedIn (post within last 90 days, ideally last 30)
- Job title contains "owner" / "operator" / "founder" / "GM" / "manager"
- Company size: 1-10 employees (we don't sell to enterprise — they need
  custom builds)

**Tools**:
- Sales Navigator: $99/mo. Required for the search filters above. Skip
  if budget is tight; LinkedIn free search can still work but is slower.
- Phantombuster / Apollo: optional for batch profile enrichment. Not
  required.

## Connection request message (template)

Keep this under 200 characters. LinkedIn's connection request box is
small and people read it like SMS.

**Template A — direct value**:
"Hi [Name], saw you run [Their Business]. We license CMRA software to
mailbox operators. Wanted to connect — happy to share what's working
for our peers."

**Template B — case-study lead** (best for warmer profiles):
"Hi [Name], we just helped a NoHo mailbox operator drop their iPostal1
take by 35%. Curious if your setup is similar. Would love to connect."

**Template C — peer signal** (for operators visibly mentioning iPostal1
in their profile or posts):
"Hi [Name], noticed you mention iPostal1 in your profile. We built our
own CMRA software because their take crushed margins. Connect?"

**Don't**:
- Open with "I'd love to connect to expand my network"
- Use the default LinkedIn message
- Try to pitch in the connection request itself
- Use any AI-generated language (people detect it instantly)

**Connection acceptance rate target**: 30-40%. If you're below 25%,
either your profile isn't credible or your message is too aggressive.

## First DM (after they accept) — the open

Send within 24-48 hours of acceptance. Not immediately (looks automated).

**Template — the soft open**:

"Hey [First Name], thanks for connecting.

Quick context — we run NOHO Mailbox in LA and license the software to
~6 other operators. Most of them came from iPostal1 / Anytime / PostScan
because the platform fees were eating margin.

I don't want to pitch you cold. Two questions to figure out if there's
even a fit:

1. How many active boxes do you currently run? (rough range is fine)
2. What software / network are you on now?

If we're not a fit, no problem — happy to send you our migration playbook
either way. We've migrated operators 6 times so we wrote down what we
learned: [link].

— [Your Name]"

**Reply rate target**: 15-25%. If below 10%, the open is too long or too
sales-y.

**Common replies you'll get**:

1. "I'm on iPostal1, ~50 boxes" → respond with the math (next section)
2. "I run my own software / spreadsheets" → ask about pain points + share
   our admin demo
3. "We're growing — call us in 6 months" → schedule a follow-up DM in 6
   months, mark in CRM, move on
4. "Not interested" → reply "Got it, all the best with [Their Business]"
   and move on. No follow-up.
5. No reply within 7 days → send the bump (next section)

---

## Second DM (the bump) — 7 days later if no reply

Keep super short. People miss DMs all the time.

"[First Name] — bumping this in case it got buried. No pressure, just
wanted to make sure you saw it. If now's not a good time, no worries.

— [Your Name]"

**Reply rate on bump**: 8-15%. Adds 50% to total reply rate.

**Don't bump twice.** If they don't reply to the bump, mark them
"unresponsive" in your CRM and move on. Try again in 6 months.

---

## Third DM (the value drop) — 14 days later if no reply

Final shot. Send a piece of value with no ask attached.

"[First Name], last note from me — I won't keep bothering you.

If it ever helps, here's our case study on how we run NOHO Mailbox with
this software stack. ~$28k MRR from boxes, ~$15k from delivery, all
managed by 2 people: [link]

If it's never relevant, no problem. Stay in touch.

— [Your Name]"

**Reply rate on value drop**: 5-10%. The case study is the unlock — they
read it, then sometimes reply 3-6 months later when their iPostal1
contract is up.

## Demo booking — when they're warm

Once a target indicates interest (asks pricing, asks for demo, says "tell
me more"):

"Happy to walk you through the platform. Real demo, not a sales pitch —
I'll show you the actual admin dashboard, member portal, dispatch
console, billing module, CMRA compliance checks. ~30 minutes.

Three times this week:

- Tuesday [date] at 10am PT
- Wednesday [date] at 2pm PT
- Thursday [date] at 11am PT

Pick one or send a time that works for you."

**Avoid Calendly links in the first booking message.** People with smaller
teams sometimes find Calendly impersonal. Hand-pick 3 slots, then move
to Calendly only if they push back.

## Migration math — for replies that mention current network

When someone replies with their box count + network, run this math
back at them:

**Template (50 boxes, on iPostal1)**:

"Quick math for 50 boxes on iPostal1:

If your customers pay ~$15/mo average:
- Gross: ~$9,000/yr from box subscriptions alone
- iPostal1's take (typical 20-40%): $1,800-$3,600/yr
- Your net: $5,400-$7,200/yr

On our platform ($299/mo flat):
- License: $3,588/yr
- Your net: $5,412/yr (at the low end of iPostal1) to ~$1,500/yr ahead
  (at the high end)

The real win is at scale: 100 boxes, 200 boxes, the math gets dramatically
better because our license stays $299 flat while their take grows with
your revenue.

Want to see the spreadsheet? I can send a custom one with your exact
numbers. Reply with your average monthly subscription per box and I'll
plug it in."

This converts ~25-40% of the math conversations into demos.

## Cadence + volume

**Daily**:
- 20-40 connection requests (Sales Nav: 35; free LinkedIn: 20)
- 10-20 first DMs (to people who accepted in the last 24-48 hours)
- 5-15 bump DMs (to people who didn't reply 7 days ago)
- 2-5 value-drop DMs (to people who didn't reply to the bump 14 days ago)

**Weekly**:
- ~150-280 connection requests sent
- ~70-140 first DMs sent
- 5-15 demos booked (target)
- 1-3 demos held (target — most reschedule once)

**Monthly**:
- ~600-1100 connections sent
- ~280-560 first DMs sent
- ~20-60 demos booked
- ~10-30 demos held
- 1-3 closes

**LTV at scale** (assuming $299/mo Solo + $799/mo Multi mix):
- Average MRR per close: ~$500
- Average LTV (24mo): ~$12,000
- 1-3 closes/mo = $12-36k LTV/mo added

## Bans + risks

LinkedIn limits connection requests to ~100/week for free accounts and
~250/week for Sales Nav. **Going above this gets you a temporary
restriction (typically 7-14 days).**

If you get rate-limited:
1. Stop sending connection requests for 7 days
2. Continue sending DMs to existing connections
3. Resume connections at 50% volume for 7 days
4. Ramp back up

**Common things that trigger restrictions**:
- Identical message to >20 people in a day (the spam filter catches this)
- Connection request to people with no shared network (low acceptance
  → high reject signal)
- Daily volume above your account's tier (250 for Sales Nav, 100 for free)

**Mitigation**:
- Vary your message wording slightly (5-10 templates rotated)
- Filter to 2nd-degree connections preferentially (higher acceptance)
- Stay within your account tier's limits

## Tracking + CRM

**Minimum tracking** (Google Sheets is fine):
- Date sent, name, company, target type (iPostal1 / Anytime / etc.)
- Connection accepted? (Y/N + date)
- First DM sent? (date)
- Reply received? (date + summary)
- Demo booked? (date)
- Outcome (Closed / Lost / Pending)

**Weekly review**:
- Connection acceptance rate (target: 30-40%)
- First DM reply rate (target: 15-25%)
- Demo conversion rate from interested replies (target: 30-40%)
- Demo-to-close rate (target: 25-35%)

**What to A/B test**:
- Connection request templates (3 templates × 50 sends each, pick winner)
- First DM open lines (the first sentence is what they read)
- Demo time slots (PT vs ET vs no-time-zone)

---

## Disqualifying signals (DO NOT pursue)

Don't waste cycles on:
- Profiles with no recent activity (90+ days)
- Profiles with under 100 connections (probably abandoned)
- Operators who post anti-automation / anti-vendor content (will not buy
  vendor software)
- Operators clearly running a side-business / not their main income
  (won't pay $299+/mo)
- Anyone in a country we don't license to yet (US only)

---

## Compliance

LinkedIn ToS forbids:
- Scraping (don't use Phantombuster's auto-message tool)
- Automation that simulates human typing (Dux-Soup, Linked Helper, etc.
  are gray-area)
- Buying / selling LinkedIn accounts

Send manually or use approved Sales Navigator features only. The risk of
losing your account is much higher than any time saved by automation.
