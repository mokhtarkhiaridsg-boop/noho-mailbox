# Press Release Templates

5 ready-to-fill press release templates for milestone events. Used
to drive PR-led organic backlinks + brand authority.

**Distribution**: PR Newswire ($350-$500 per release for narrow
distribution, $700-$2,000 for wider) OR free distribution via local
LA business newsletters + LinkedIn announcement + blog post.

For a CMRA + small-business operation, free distribution is usually
sufficient. PR Newswire makes sense only for major milestones.

---

## Template 1: Funding / Major Milestone

**When to use**: $1M+ ARR milestone, major investment, partnership
with named brand, etc.

```
FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE

NOHO Mailbox [Hits Milestone / Announces Investment / Partners with
X] — [Headline detail in <80 chars]

NORTH HOLLYWOOD, CA — [Date] — NOHO Mailbox, a [adjective] commercial
mail receiving agency (CMRA) and same-day delivery operator based in
North Hollywood, California, today announced [the milestone].

[Paragraph 1: 2-3 sentences with the news. The "what" and "when."
Lead with numbers if possible.]

[Paragraph 2: 2-3 sentences explaining why this matters in the
broader market context. Avoid jargon.]

[Paragraph 3: A pull quote from the founder. Keep it under 50 words.]
"[Quote — describe what the milestone means + why it matters to
customers]" said [Founder Name], founder of NOHO Mailbox.

[Paragraph 4: 2-3 sentences on what comes next. Include a forward-
looking commitment.]

About NOHO Mailbox

NOHO Mailbox provides commercial mail receiving services, same-day
courier delivery, business launch services, and CMRA software
licensing from its 5062 Lankershim Blvd location in North Hollywood,
California. Founded in 2022, the company processes approximately 500
active mailboxes and operates same-day delivery routes throughout
greater Los Angeles. Its software platform is licensed to other CMRA
operators across multiple US states. Learn more at nohomailbox.org.

Press contact:
[Press Name]
[Press Email]
(818) 506-7744

###
```

## Distribution checklist

For ANY release:

1. **Pre-release (1-2 days before)**:
   - Email to top 3 LA local journalists (LA Times business, LA
     Business Journal, LA Daily News, San Fernando Valley Sun)
   - Email to top 3 industry journalists (Mailbox Industry News,
     Postal Times, MultiChannel Merchant)
   - Embargoed copy with "DO NOT PUBLISH UNTIL [date/time]"

2. **Day of release**:
   - Distribute via PR Newswire (if budget) OR
   - Post on company blog
   - Post LinkedIn announcement (founder personal account + company)
   - Tweet announcement (if Twitter active)
   - Email to existing customer list (newsletter)
   - Email to affiliate partners (they may amplify)

3. **Day after**:
   - Pitch direct to 5-10 podcast hosts in adjacent industries
   - Pitch to 2-3 newsletter authors who cover small business / LLC
     formation / e-commerce / CMRA industry
   - Update About page + Press page

## What makes a press release actually get picked up

Most press releases get ignored. The ones that get picked up share:

1. **Genuine news**, not a marketing milestone. "We hit $1M ARR" is
   barely newsworthy. "Our software is now licensed in 6 states" is
   borderline. "We launched a new product that solves [specific
   problem]" is news.

2. **Specific names and numbers**. "Helped Sarah Smith of Burbank
   grow her Etsy shop from $2k/mo to $14k/mo." > "Helped a customer
   grow their business."

3. **Local angle for local outlets**. LA Times Business cares about
   LA. The San Fernando Valley Sun cares about the SFV. Tailor the
   pitch.

4. **Quote-able lines**. Journalists are looking for sound bites.
   Give them 2-3 short quotes (under 50 words each) they can pull
   directly.

5. **Visuals**. Always include 2-3 high-res photos: storefront,
   founder headshot, customer (if applicable). Most coverage requires
   a photo.

6. **Easy contact**. Press contact email + phone in the release.
   Respond to journalists within 1 hour during business hours.

## Press kit assets

Maintain a single page at `nohomailbox.org/press` (we don&apos;t have
this yet — build when first needed) with:

- Company description (1-paragraph + 3-paragraph version)
- Founder bio (1-paragraph)
- High-res logos (PNG + SVG, light + dark backgrounds)
- High-res storefront photos (interior + exterior)
- Founder headshots
- Recent press coverage links
- Press contact email
- All press releases (chronological)

Keep this updated. Journalists need it accessible 24/7.

---

## What NOT to do

1. **Don&apos;t spam press releases.** 1-2 per quarter at most. More
   than that and you train journalists to ignore you.
2. **Don&apos;t exaggerate.** "First in the industry" claims that
   aren&apos;t actually first will get you a correction notice and
   damage credibility.
3. **Don&apos;t use marketing language.** "Best-in-class," "industry-
   leading," "revolutionary" — strip them. Journalists use red pens
   on these words.
4. **Don&apos;t bury the news in the third paragraph.** First sentence
   should answer "what&apos;s the news?"
5. **Don&apos;t forget the boilerplate.** Every release ends with
   the company description + press contact.
