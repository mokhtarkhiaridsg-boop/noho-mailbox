# Customer Personas

5 detailed personas representing 80%+ of NOHO Mailbox revenue. Use
these for copywriting, ad targeting, content prioritization, product
decisions.

**Each persona includes**: demographics, day-to-day, pain points,
buying triggers, objections, what we sell them, where to reach them.

---

## Persona 1: Maya — The Etsy / Shopify side-hustler

**Demographics**:
- 28-38, female, lives in LA County
- Day job: marketing / design / education / healthcare
- Side income: $5k-$25k/year on Etsy or Shopify
- Mostly handcrafted products (jewelry, candles, prints, paper goods)
- Income relies on weekends + evenings

**Day-to-day**:
- Wakes up early, checks Etsy orders before day job
- Side-business work happens 7pm-11pm + weekends
- Uses a stack of platforms: Etsy + Shopify + Instagram + (sometimes)
  TikTok
- Ships from her apartment using printed labels
- Stresses about taxes, address privacy, "should I get an LLC?"

**Pain points**:
- Home address showing on Etsy listings + customer disputes
- 1099-K from Etsy / Shopify going to her apartment (visible to
  roommates / spouse)
- Bank refused her business account application using a P.O. Box
- Confused about LLC vs sole prop — gets conflicting advice from
  YouTube
- Anxious about California's $800/yr franchise tax

**Buying triggers**:
- Reaches $20k revenue + family member asks "are you protected if
  someone sues you?"
- Etsy verification fails the first time (asks for non-PO Box address)
- Bank rejection on business account
- A YouTube video she watched scared her about CA LLC requirements
- Tax season — realizes she needs to clean up her business address

**Common objections**:
- "$50 / 3 months feels expensive — I&apos;m only making $5k/year"
- "I don&apos;t want to commit to anything I might cancel"
- "I&apos;m not sure if I really need an LLC yet"

**What we sell her**:
- Primary: Mailbox plan, Basic ($50/3mo) or Business ($80/3mo)
- Secondary: Eventually $2k Bundle when revenue clears $30k

**Where to reach her**:
- Etsy seller subreddit (r/Etsy, r/EtsySellers)
- Etsy seller YouTube creators
- Local LA Etsy meetup groups
- Pinterest / Instagram (visual-first platforms)

**Best content for her**:
- "Should I form an LLC for my Etsy shop?" (we have this)
- "Etsy verification: how to pass on the first try"
- "Privacy on Etsy — what your address shows customers"

## Persona 2: David — The solo attorney scaling out

**Demographics**:
- 35-50, male, lives in West Side / Glendale / Pasadena
- Solo attorney for 3-8 years
- Practice areas: immigration / family / personal injury / general
  practice
- Revenue: $200k-$600k/year
- Office: shared coworking suite OR dedicated 1-3 person office

**Day-to-day**:
- Court runs at Stanley Mosk + LA Superior + IJ courthouses
- Client meetings at coffee shops / Zoom / office
- Constant filing deadlines (everything has a 30-day clock)
- Loose paralegal support but not full-time admin

**Pain points**:
- Court runs eat 30-45 minutes per filing — at $400/hr partner time
  that's $200-300 of opportunity cost
- Process servers and runners are unreliable / no-show
- Existing courier services don&apos;t do walk-the-document-back
- Lost a filing once to FedEx misroute — career-threatening

**Buying triggers**:
- Just lost a deadline because a runner no-showed
- Practice growing (more case files = more court runs)
- Hiring his first paralegal but doesn&apos;t want them on courier
  duty
- Office moves to less-central location (more travel time)

**Common objections**:
- "DoorDash is $10 — why are you $9.75?"
- "I have my own paralegal who does runs"
- "Same-day usually means by 5pm — I sometimes need 2pm filings"

**What we sell him**:
- Primary: Same-day delivery, recurring routes (5-15 runs/week)
- Secondary: Mailbox plan if he wants a separate business address

**Where to reach him**:
- LinkedIn (search "solo attorney" + LA filter)
- Local bar association events
- LA County Bar Association directory
- Court runner forums (state-specific lawyer groups)

**Best content for him**:
- "Same-day courier for solo attorneys in LA"
- "Court filing service comparison: us vs DoorDash vs FedEx"

## Persona 3: Sarah — The newly-going-independent realtor

**Demographics**:
- 32-45, female, lives in LA County
- 2-5 years as W-2 agent at Coldwell Banker / Compass / similar
- Recently went independent (or considering it)
- Revenue: $80k-$300k commission/year
- Has California Real Estate License (DRE)

**Day-to-day**:
- Showings scattered across LA (lots of driving)
- Open houses on weekends
- Constantly moving keys, lockboxes, and signed paperwork
- DRE-licensed, so requires a real CA business address (P.O. Box
  rejected by DRE)

**Pain points**:
- DRE rejects P.O. Box for the licensee&apos;s office address
- Used to lean on the brokerage for signage, business address,
  insurance — now has to do it herself
- Lockboxes + earnest checks need same-day pickup / drop-off
- Wants to look professional without paying brokerage overhead
- Confused about LLC vs DBA vs S-corp election

**Buying triggers**:
- Just left her brokerage (the trigger event)
- DRE renewal coming up + needs a real business address
- Realized she has zero brand without the brokerage&apos;s — needs
  her own logo + website

**Common objections**:
- "I'm probably going to keep some affiliation with [brokerage] —
  not sure I need this yet"
- "$2k for the bundle is more than my marketing budget"

**What we sell her**:
- Primary: $2k Business Launch Bundle (LLC + brand + website +
  address)
- Secondary: Same-day delivery for lockboxes / earnest checks

**Where to reach her**:
- LinkedIn (search "real estate agent" + "LA" + "independent")
- Real estate going-independent Facebook groups
- DRE renewal mailing lists
- "Going independent realtor" YouTube creators

**Best content for her**:
- "Realtor going independent in California — checklist" (we have
  this)
- "What DRE accepts for licensee address (and what it doesn't)"

## Persona 4: Mike — The CMRA operator considering switching

**Demographics**:
- 40-65, male, lives in Texas / Florida / Nevada / California
- Owns 1-3 location CMRA / pack-and-ship store
- Revenue: $80k-$400k/year (gross), with iPostal1 / Anytime /
  PostScan taking a 20-40% cut
- Background: small-business owner (often former retail / hospitality)

**Day-to-day**:
- Opens storefront at 9am, closes at 6pm
- Sorts inbound mail, helps walk-in customers
- Manages 1-3 staff people
- Pays iPostal1 / Anytime monthly invoices that he hates

**Pain points**:
- Network fees eating margin — gets visibly angry at the iPostal1
  invoice
- Wants to add same-day delivery but the platform won&apos;t support
  it
- White-label promised but never delivered by the network
- Customer-acquisition feels like working for the network's brand,
  not his own

**Buying triggers**:
- iPostal1 raised pricing OR added a fee
- Recently passed 75 boxes (the math definitely works at this scale)
- Heard about NOHO Mailbox at a regional CMRA conference
- His own developer son told him "this is the right move"

**Common objections**:
- "Migration sounds painful"
- "$299/mo is more than I'm paying iPostal1 today" (sometimes true at
  small box counts)
- "What if your software has bugs?"

**What we sell him**:
- Primary: CMRA SaaS license ($299-$1,499/mo)
- Secondary: Migration services (included in license)

**Where to reach him**:
- LinkedIn (search "mailbox business" or specific networks like
  "iPostal1 operator" + decision-maker titles)
- USPS-CMRA conferences
- Pack-and-ship industry associations
- B2B podcasts in postal / CMRA / virtual mailbox space

**Best content for him**:
- "CMRA Operator Switching Guide" (sales kit doc 25)
- "Migration math at 100 boxes"
- "Why we left iPostal1 (operator perspective)"

## Cross-persona insights

### What all 5 personas share

1. **Privacy concern**: home address showing up where it shouldn&apos;t
2. **Trust signal need**: a real LA address that platforms / banks /
   government accept
3. **Avoidance of complexity**: would prefer ONE vendor that handles
   the whole thing vs 4-5 vendors
4. **Skepticism of marketing language**: respond to specific numbers
   + real customer stories vs hype

### What we should NOT do for any persona

1. Push the most expensive plan first
2. Use jargon (CMRA / 1583 / etc.) without explaining
3. Pretend we&apos;re cheaper than we are
4. Promise outcomes we can&apos;t guarantee
5. Use AI-feeling templated language

## How to use these personas

### For copywriting
- Pick the persona before writing
- Imagine the SPECIFIC person reading your copy
- Use their language, their objections, their concerns
- Skip language they wouldn&apos;t care about

### For ad targeting
- Build separate ad campaigns per persona
- Use persona-specific keywords (doc 19)
- Use persona-specific landing pages (doc /for/[persona])
- Different creative per persona

### For content prioritization
- Top-of-funnel content: targets persona pain points
- Mid-funnel content: addresses persona objections
- Bottom-funnel: case studies featuring similar personas

### For product decisions
- "Would [persona] use this?" is a fair gut-check
- Some features serve only 1 persona — fine if that persona is
  high-value
- Features that don&apos;t serve any of the 5 personas: probably
  wrong investment

---

## What this template ISN&apos;T

- Not a substitute for actual customer research. Talk to 20 real
  customers per quarter to validate / update.
- Not the only personas. There are edge cases (large enterprise,
  niche industries) — they&apos;re just not the 80%.
- Not static. Personas evolve. Re-validate annually.

---

## Annual persona review (15-min task)

Once a year:

1. Pull last year&apos;s closed customers
2. Map each to one of the 5 personas
3. Calculate: which persona drove the most revenue? Which has highest
   LTV? Highest CAC?
4. Reallocate: which persona deserves more marketing investment?
   Which should we de-emphasize?

If a persona drives <5% of revenue for 2+ consecutive years, retire
it. If a new persona is emerging that&apos;s not covered, add a 6th.
