# Seasonal Revenue Calendar

When each product line peaks. When to plan capacity, push marketing,
and prep operations.

**Use case**: planning quarterly content calendars, hiring decisions,
ad budget allocation, capacity ramps.

---

## January — "New Year, New Business" surge

### What spikes
- LLC formation searches (people forming "this year")
- Mailbox plan signups (resolution-driven)
- $2k Bundle inquiries

### What slows
- Holiday gift / flowers same-day delivery
- General storefront walk-ins (post-holiday lull)

### What to do
- **Marketing focus**: content + paid ads pushing LLC formation.
  Run "$800 trap" reminder campaign (CA franchise tax due dates).
- **Capacity prep**: founder + sales personally handle bundle
  inquiries (highest-AOV inbound surge of the year)
- **Operations**: prep for tax-form scanning surge in late January
- **Content**: blog posts on tax planning, LLC formation, EIN
  application

### Specific tactics
- Late January: 1099-NEC arrives for affiliates / B2B customers.
  Email reminding them to update their address with us so 1099-Ks
  forward correctly.
- Newsletter: "5 tax form deadlines you can&apos;t miss"
- LinkedIn: founder personal post about Q1 priorities

---

## February — Tax season operational peak

### What spikes
- Mail volume (W-2s, 1099-NECs, 1099-Ks all arriving)
- Mail scanning requests (taxes mean docs need to be in customer hands)
- Customer support volume (questions about address changes, missing
  forms, etc.)

### What slows
- New customer acquisition (people are busy with their existing
  taxes)

### What to do
- **Operations focus**: storefront prep for ~30-50% volume spike
  Feb 1 - Feb 28
- **Marketing**: keep low-cost organic content; pause aggressive
  paid ads
- **Customer support**: extra hands for the scanning + "where&apos;s
  my form?" questions

### Specific tactics
- Pre-staff: hire seasonal helper for Feb if you anticipate volume
  spike
- Communications: send pre-emptive email to all customers about
  expected mail volume + scan queue timing
- Inventory: extra envelopes, scanning supplies, Form 1583 packets

---

## March — Tax filing pre-deadline rush

### What spikes
- LLC formation (people realizing they need one before filing taxes)
- $2k Bundle inquiries (especially for sole-prop → LLC conversions)
- Customer questions about S-corp election (Form 2553 deadline March
  15)

### What to do
- **Marketing focus**: LLC formation + S-corp election content
- **Sales focus**: bundle inquiries get personal first-touch
- **Operations**: more notarizations needed for Form 1583 (free with
  Business + Premium plans)

### Specific tactics
- Blog post: "S-corp election deadline: what you need to know"
- LinkedIn: founder personal post about CA tax deadlines
- Email: existing customers about the bundle (cross-sell)

---

## April — Tax day + post-tax slow-down

### What spikes
- LLC formation (last-minute "I need to form before Apr 15")
- Bundle inquiries (panic-driven)

### What slows (post April 15)
- Most acquisition activity (people are exhausted)
- B2B same-day delivery (less urgent paperwork)

### What to do
- **Marketing focus**: deadline-driven through Apr 15
- **Content**: prep for May calendar (Mother&apos;s Day push)
- **Operations**: standard rhythm

### Specific tactics
- Apr 15: "Last-minute LLC + EIN — we can deliver in 14 days"
- Newsletter: post-tax wrap-up + what to do for next year
- Begin Mother&apos;s Day florist prep (May 11-12)

---

## May — Mother&apos;s Day same-day delivery surge

### What spikes
- Florist same-day delivery (Mother&apos;s Day = peak florist day,
  3-5x normal volume)
- Same-day delivery dispatch volume

### What to do
- **Operations focus**: maximum-capacity Mother&apos;s Day weekend
  (Saturday + Sunday)
- **Driver staffing**: pre-hire extra drivers 3 weeks before
- **Marketing**: florist outreach in late April

### Specific tactics
- Pre-Mother&apos;s Day: outreach to LA-area florists (sales kit doc
  11 mother&apos;s day sprint)
- Capacity: schedule 3-4 drivers Saturday + Sunday vs usual 1-2
- Pricing: do NOT discount (floristas pay premium for reliability)

---

## June — Wedding season + summer school out

### What spikes
- Same-day delivery for wedding-related (florist, signage, gifts)
- Mailbox plans for digital nomads (people leaving for summer
  travel)
- Real estate same-day delivery (peak home-buying season)

### What to do
- **Marketing**: digital nomad + travel-friendly mailbox content
- **Content**: real estate-specific same-day delivery content
- **Operations**: prep for slightly higher delivery volume

### Specific tactics
- TikTok / IG content: travel + nomad angle
- LinkedIn: real estate agent outreach
- Blog post: "Best mailing address for digital nomads (2026 update)"

---

## July — Slowdown month

### What slows
- Most acquisition (vacation season)
- Same-day delivery (B2B summer-light)

### What to do
- **Strategic focus**: catch up on infrastructure, content, planning
- **Marketing**: maintain organic but cut paid ads spend
- **Operations**: standard rhythm

### Specific tactics
- Annual planning prep (sales kit doc 34 — even if you don&apos;t do
  full plan until December, July is where you start collecting data)
- Content: evergreen content (long-form blog posts, YouTube videos)
- Internal: SOP / playbook documentation

---

## August — Back-to-school + family business surge

### What spikes
- Mailbox plans (parents starting Etsy / side hustles for the
  school year)
- $2k Bundle (family business launches)
- Online coach / consultant sign-ups (pre-Q4 launches)

### What to do
- **Marketing**: target side-hustle / Etsy / coach personas
- **Content**: "Starting an Etsy / Shopify shop for the school year"
- **Operations**: standard

### Specific tactics
- Newsletter: "Side hustle in Q4 — what you need"
- LinkedIn: targeting coaches, consultants, online educators
- Reddit / Quora: r/Etsy, r/smallbusiness

---

## September — Q4 planning + LLC pre-deadline

### What spikes
- LLC formation (forming for Q4 launches before tax year ends)
- $2k Bundle (Etsy/Amazon/Shopify holiday-season prep)
- Mailbox plans (Q4 commerce ramp-up)

### What to do
- **Marketing focus**: heavy. Q4 is the biggest acquisition window
  of the year.
- **Content**: holiday-prep content for e-commerce sellers
- **Operations**: prep for Q4 mail volume surge

### Specific tactics
- Paid ads: increase budget 30-50% for Q4
- Newsletter: holiday planning content
- Affiliate program: recruit aggressively (Q4 is when they earn
  most)

---

## October — Q4 commerce ramp-up

### What spikes
- E-commerce same-day delivery (last-mile prep)
- Mailbox plans (Etsy / Amazon sellers ramping for holidays)
- Mail volume (commercial mail volume goes up)

### What to do
- **Operations focus**: capacity for mail surge
- **Marketing**: e-commerce-specific content
- **Sales**: Q4 acquisition push

### Specific tactics
- Blog post: "Etsy holiday season checklist"
- LinkedIn: B2B e-commerce content
- Operations: extra scanning capacity
- Affiliate: top affiliates get custom Q4 assets

---

## November — Holiday operational peak begins

### What spikes
- Same-day delivery for retail (gift surges starting)
- Mail volume (massive)
- Customer support (everything-is-urgent vibe)

### What slows
- New mailbox / bundle acquisition (people are heads-down on Q4)

### What to do
- **Operations focus**: peak-capacity through end of December
- **Marketing**: less new acquisition, more existing-customer cross-
  sell
- **Sales**: switch from outbound to inbound triage focus

### Specific tactics
- Black Friday / Cyber Monday: small storefront promotion (10% off
  Premium plan, etc.) — only if conversion math works
- Email: existing customers (cross-sell same-day delivery if they
  haven&apos;t used it)
- Operations: 7-day-a-week dispatch coverage from late Nov to Dec

---

## December — Holiday peak + year-end

### What spikes
- Same-day delivery (retail + last-mile gifts)
- Mail volume (gift-card mailings, retail returns)
- Last-minute LLC formation (year-end-deadline-driven)

### What slows
- New customer acquisition (people are at parties)
- Marketing (audiences not paying attention)

### What to do
- **Operations focus**: maximum capacity Dec 1-22
- **Marketing**: minimal new content; lean on existing pipeline
- **Strategic**: annual planning (sales kit doc 34) starts Dec 26

### Specific tactics
- Pre-Christmas (Dec 18-22): final delivery push, pre-staff drivers
- Christmas week: lighter operations, founder takes 5 days off
- Dec 26-Jan 5: annual planning + Q1 prep

## Annual planning rhythm

| Quarter | Theme | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| Q1 (Jan-Mar) | Tax season + new-business surge | Acquisition |
| Q2 (Apr-Jun) | Wedding + summer + tax wrap | Marketing infrastructure |
| Q3 (Jul-Sep) | Slowdown + Q4 prep | Strategic / content / SOPs |
| Q4 (Oct-Dec) | Holiday peak | Operations + retention |

---

## Day-by-day priority shifts

Specific dates to mark on the calendar:

- **Jan 15**: Q4-of-prior-year quarterly tax payment deadline
- **Mar 15**: S-corp election deadline (Form 2553)
- **Apr 15**: Personal + LLC tax deadline
- **May 11-12 (varies)**: Mother&apos;s Day weekend
- **Jun 15**: Q2 quarterly tax payment
- **Jul 4 (and surrounding)**: holiday slow-down
- **Sep 15**: Q3 quarterly tax payment
- **Nov 1**: California Statement of Information renewal window opens
  for many LLCs
- **Nov 15** (variable): Black Friday week begins
- **Dec 31**: year-end → annual report deadlines for some states

## Capacity planning by quarter

### Q1 (high volume + acquisition)
- Founder capacity: 80% on customer-facing work, 20% on building
- Staff: full team active; possibly 1 seasonal hire for Feb
- Inventory: extra Form 1583, scanning supplies

### Q2 (medium acquisition, wedding spike May)
- Founder capacity: 60% customer-facing, 40% on infrastructure /
  content
- Staff: 1-2 extra drivers Mother&apos;s Day weekend
- Inventory: standard

### Q3 (slowest quarter)
- Founder capacity: 40% customer-facing, 60% on building
- Staff: standard (consider summer-vacation rotation)
- Inventory: standard
- Strategic: annual planning prep

### Q4 (peak)
- Founder capacity: 90% customer-facing, 10% emergency-only build
- Staff: maximum (1-2 extra drivers, possibly 1 extra storefront
  helper)
- Inventory: 2x normal supplies, extra envelopes, packing tape, tape
  guns
- Operations: 7-day coverage Nov 25 - Dec 22

## Seasonal marketing budget allocation

If your annual marketing budget is $X, allocate roughly:

- Q1 (Jan-Mar): 35% of annual (peak acquisition)
- Q2 (Apr-Jun): 20% (steady, with Mother&apos;s Day push)
- Q3 (Jul-Sep): 15% (low; focus on building infrastructure)
- Q4 (Oct-Dec): 30% (push pre-Black-Friday)

If budget is $20k/year:
- Q1: $7k
- Q2: $4k
- Q3: $3k
- Q4: $6k

Within each quarter, allocate further by month (e.g., Q1 → Jan $3k,
Feb $2k, Mar $2k).

---

## What this calendar ISN&apos;T

- Not a guarantee. Seasonal patterns shift; we run the math each year.
- Not exhaustive. There are weekly / monthly cycles (1st-of-month
  invoicing surge, end-of-quarter rushes for SaaS) that aren&apos;t
  captured here.
- Not for crisis years. If a major external event happens (recession,
  natural disaster, regulatory change), throw the calendar out.

---

## How to use this calendar

1. **Pin to your wall** (or digital equivalent). Reference monthly.
2. **Plan content calendar** (sales kit doc 30) around it. Tax-related
   content goes Jan-Mar.
3. **Plan budget** quarterly using the allocation framework above.
4. **Plan hiring** — Q3 is the cheapest time to hire because many
   competitors are slow.
5. **Plan time off** — Q3 is the right time. Don&apos;t take Q1 or
   Q4 off if you can avoid it.
