# NOHO Mailbox — Sales Strategy

**Goal:** drive paid customer growth on two lanes — **same-day delivery** (high frequency, low ticket) and **business solutions** (low frequency, high ticket).

**Owner-operator constraint:** every channel must be runnable by one person from the storefront with no automation cost over $50/mo unless it converts.

---

## 1. Positioning vs the LA courier market

The big players are **ClockWork Express** (25-year medical/OSHA), **California Courier Services** (24/7 enterprise), **Ways Messenger**, **Jet Delivery**. They sell to hospitals, law-firm chains, and corporate accounts.

NOHO Mailbox is not them and shouldn't try to be. Your edge:

| Their weakness | Your win |
|---|---|
| $25–$60 minimums + dispatcher | $5 flat in NoHo zone, no membership |
| Phone tree, ticket numbers | Real human, walk-in storefront |
| Medical-only or B2B-only | Walk-in, B2B, e-com all under one roof |
| No notary / mailbox / shipping bundle | All of it in one stop, one invoice |

**Tagline candidates** (pick one and use everywhere):
- "Your block's delivery guy."
- "Five bucks. Same hour. Real human."
- "The neighborhood's mailroom."

## 2. Two ICPs, two lanes

### Lane A — Same-day delivery
**Primary ICP:** small businesses inside the NoHo + Studio City + Burbank delivery radius that need 1–10 deliveries/week and currently overpay a regional courier or do it themselves.

Top 7 verticals (ranked by frequency × pain × close speed):

1. **Solo/small attorneys** (court runs, originals, exhibits) — 2–10 runs/week, $15–$40 saved per run
2. **Real estate agents & escrow** (keys, earnest checks, signed contracts) — peak transaction days, 1–5 runs/wk
3. **Florists / bakeries** during peak holidays (Valentine's, Mother's Day, Christmas) — overflow, can't keep up
4. **Print shops & sign makers** — last-mile when client can't pick up
5. **Small medical/dental** (single-doc practices, NOT chains — they use ClockWork) — labs, scripts, dentures
6. **Boutiques & small e-com sellers** — local Shopify/Etsy with same-zip buyers
7. **Notaries & process servers** — overflow / backup

### Lane B — Business solutions ($2k bundle, $1.2k/mo retainer)
**Primary ICP:** first-time formers and rebrand candidates within driving distance of NoHo.

Top 5 verticals:

1. **Existing Business-tier mailbox customers** — already trust you, already paying you, easiest upsell on planet
2. **Realtors going independent** — forming DBA + brand + site
3. **Etsy / Amazon / Shopify sellers** — need real LLC + EIN + brand for trademark/wholesale
4. **Immigrant-founded service businesses** (mechanics, contractors, salons, restaurants) — community trust, in-person preferred (matches Khiari memory note)
5. **Side-hustlers going full-time** — w-2 escapees, content creators, consultants

## 4. Funnel & metrics

Define one north-star number per lane and review weekly:

- **Lane A (delivery):** *new repeating customers per week* (= placed ≥ 2 orders within 14 days). Target: 5/wk by week 8.
- **Lane B (business solutions):** *qualified consults booked per week*. Target: 3/wk by week 8. Conversion target: 1 in 3 books → $2k bundle close.

Per-lead cost ceiling:
- Lane A: $15 max blended (each new repeating customer is worth ~$60/mo gross)
- Lane B: $250 max blended (each closed bundle is $2k one-time + retainer potential)

## 5. 60-day execution rhythm

- **Week 1:** Hot-list outbound (100 contacts), hand-walked. Existing-customer upsell email/SMS. Partner-program one-pager printed and distributed in person to 10 CPAs/lawyers.
- **Week 2:** GBP + Yelp + Apple Maps + Nextdoor optimization shipped. First 2 SEO blog posts live.
- **Week 3:** Follow-up on Week 1 outbound. First partner-referral check-ins. 3 more SEO posts.
- **Week 4–6:** Iterate on the highest-converting message. Expand outbound list. Consider paid only if lead flow is below target.
- **Week 8:** Review north-star numbers. Cut what's not working. Double the spend on what is.
