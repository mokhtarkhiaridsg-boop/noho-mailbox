## The 8-Step Filing Process

1. **Pick a name.** Search the CA SOS database
   (bizfileonline.sos.ca.gov/search/business). Name must include "LLC,"
   "L.L.C.," or "Limited Liability Company." Avoid restricted words
   (bank, insurance, trust) without proper licensing.

2. **Pick a registered agent.** Real California physical address required.
   P.O. Boxes rejected. You can be your own agent if you have a published
   CA address. Most non-CA owners hire a service.

3. **File LLC-1 (Articles of Organization).** $70 at bizfileonline.sos.ca.gov.
   Online: 5-15 business days. Expedited (24 hours): +$15. Same-day: +$350.

4. **Get your EIN.** Free at IRS.gov. Online application takes 5-10
   minutes if you have a Social Security Number. By mail: 4-6 weeks.

5. **File LLC-12 (Statement of Information).** Within 90 days of
   formation. $20. Then biennially (every 2 years).

6. **Pay $800 minimum franchise tax.** Due 15th day of 4th month after
   formation. Plus optional income-based fee on revenue over $250k.

7. **Open a business bank account.** Bring: stamped LLC-1, EIN letter,
   government ID, registered agent address. Major banks (Chase, BofA,
   Wells, Mercury, Novo) accept our 5062 Lankershim address with a
   notarized Form 1583.

8. **Get appropriate licenses.** City business license (mandatory in
   most CA cities). Seller's permit if selling tangible goods (apply
   to CDTFA, free). Industry-specific licenses (DRE, CSLB, etc.) if
   applicable.

## When You DO Need an LLC

- Selling physical products that could harm someone (food, candles,
  cosmetics, soaps, kids' products, anything with electrical/heat)
- Revenue approaching $50k+ with personal assets at risk
- Selling wholesale to retailers (B2B contracts in play)
- Service businesses where clients could sue over results (coaching,
  consulting, real estate, healthcare)
- Multi-owner businesses (partnerships need legal structure)

## When You DON'T Need an LLC Yet

- Selling only digital products with no liability risk
- Revenue under $20k/year and you're testing
- Hobby business with no growth plan
- You're salaried elsewhere and just have a side gig

For sole props with under $20k revenue, the $800 annual franchise tax
often eats more than the LLC saves.

## Common Mistakes

**Mistake 1**: Naming the LLC after yourself ("John Smith LLC"). Hard
to rebrand if the business pivots. Use a brandable name.

**Mistake 2**: Filing without an Operating Agreement. CA doesn't legally
require one but courts use the default state rules if you don't have
one. Always have one — even a single-member template works.

**Mistake 3**: Using your home address. Privacy issue (your address goes
on county and state records). Most banks require a verifiable address
for business accounts; some flag home addresses.

**Mistake 4**: Forming the LLC before you have a bank account ready.
Forming the LLC starts the franchise tax clock. If you take 6 months
to actually open the bank account, you've burned 6 months of $800.

**Mistake 5**: Filing in Wyoming or Nevada when you operate in California.
You'll have to register the WY/NV LLC as a foreign LLC in CA anyway.
Double the agent fees, double the filings, no real savings unless you
also need anonymity.

**Mistake 6**: Skipping the EIN. You'll need it eventually for banking,
tax forms, and 1099s. Just do it now — it's free and takes 10 minutes.

## Real-Address Note

California requires a real California physical address for:
- The LLC's principal office
- The registered agent
- DRE / CSLB / professional licenses (these reject CMRA addresses)

P.O. Boxes are rejected for the LLC filing. Most CMRA addresses
(commercial mail receiving agencies) work for the LLC and registered
agent. Some professional licenses specifically require a real operating
office, not a CMRA.
