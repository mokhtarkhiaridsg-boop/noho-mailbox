# First 90 Days Execution Roadmap

The exact sequence to execute the sales kit. Day-by-day for the first
30 days, week-by-week for days 31-90.

**Prereq**: deploy is live (run `vercel deploy --prod` first thing).
Sitemap submitted to Google Search Console. Newsletter list set up.

**Time budget**: 2 hours/day for first 30 days, 1 hour/day after that.

**Morning (45 min)**:

Don&apos;t skip rest days. Burnout is the #1 reason solo execution

## Week 3-4: first conversions

### Activities increase

Volume targets for week 3-4:
- LinkedIn outbound: 25-30 connection requests/day
- Reddit / Quora answers: 1-2/week
- Blog posts: 1/week (use templates from doc 18 + existing posts as
  base)
- Newsletter: weekly, Friday send

### What to watch for

**First lead**: someone fills out a form on /business-solutions or
/for-cmra-operators. CRITICAL to first-touch within 1 hour (use doc
31 templates).

**First demo booking**: a CMRA operator from LinkedIn outreach books
a demo. Use doc 29 demo walkthrough script. Don&apos;t freelance —
follow the script.

**First close**: most likely a mailbox plan signup ($50/3mo). Track
attribution in spreadsheet (which channel did they come from?).

### Friday weekly review (every Friday)

Use doc 32 metrics dashboard. 12 numbers, 30 minutes.

If lead→close rate is 0% after week 4, something is broken in your
inbound flow. Diagnose:
- Are you actually responding to forms? (Check inbox + spam folder)
- Are your responses good? (Read the actual emails you sent)
- Is the website conversion broken? (Test a form submit yourself)

## Week 5-8: SEO compounding starts

By week 5, Google has crawled most of the new content. You should
see:
- 200-500 organic visits / day (up from 50-100 in week 1-2)
- Long-tail searches matching specific blog posts and tools
- Direct landing-page traffic from Google for some pages

### Activity adjustments

- **Increase content cadence**: 2 blog posts / week (up from 1).
  Pull from sales kit doc 30 calendar.
- **A/B test paid ads**: $300-500 budget on the highest-intent
  campaigns from doc 19 (LLC formation + mailbox plans).
- **Affiliate recruitment**: post to LinkedIn / Twitter that the
  affiliate program is open (doc 27 assets).

### What "compound" looks like

Each piece of content keeps generating organic visits forever. Your
"content cost" stays flat (same time per post) but content
contribution to revenue grows.

By week 8 you should see your first 3-5 paying customers attributed
to organic SEO traffic (people who found you via Google, not direct).

## Week 9-12: iterate based on data

By now you have 60 days of execution data. Friday metrics reviews
should reveal patterns:

### What's working
- Best lead-source channel by close rate
- Best blog posts by traffic + conversions
- Best LinkedIn post types by engagement

### What's not working
- Channels with no closes after 2 months — kill or change
- Blog posts with no traffic — review SEO basics + retitle / rewrite
- Email sequences with low reply rates — A/B test new subject lines

### Strategic decisions

Quarter-end (around day 90), make calls:

1. **Double down**: which channel deserves more time / budget?
2. **Kill**: what should we stop doing?
3. **Hire**: are you maxed out on capacity? (Sales kit doc 36)
4. **Pivot**: any persona / product underperforming so badly we
   should de-emphasize?

Write a 1-page review per doc 34 quarterly review template.

## Where most people fail

After helping multiple founders execute similar plans, here&apos;s
where it usually goes wrong:

### 1. Skipping days

The 30-min/day playbook only works if it&apos;s actually 30 min/day.
Skipping 2-3 days a week breaks the compounding. If you&apos;re going
to skip, pre-commit: "I&apos;ll do this 4 days a week (Mon-Thu),
weekends off." That&apos;s sustainable. Random skips aren&apos;t.

### 2. Pivoting too fast

After 4 weeks, a channel might be quiet. That&apos;s not the channel
failing — that&apos;s SEO indexing time. Don&apos;t pivot before 90
days unless something is OBVIOUSLY broken.

### 3. Trying to do everything

You CANNOT execute all 37 sales kit docs simultaneously. The 30-min
daily playbook (doc 22) is the focus. Use other docs as references
when needed. Don&apos;t try to be the affiliate manager AND the
LinkedIn DMer AND the content writer AND the inbound triage person
AND the demo closer all at once.

### 4. Not first-touching leads fast enough

The single biggest predictor of close rate is first-touch time. If
a lead waits 24 hours for a reply, conversion drops 80%. Set up
phone notifications on the admin dashboard. Reply to Tier A leads
within 1 hour, period.

### 5. Treating outreach as performance art

If your LinkedIn DMs are all 200-word essays, they&apos;ll fail. The
templates in doc 21 are short on purpose. Long DMs from strangers
get ignored.

## When to escalate / change course

Trigger to revisit the plan (don&apos;t wait until day 90):

- **Day 30**: zero closes. Something fundamental is wrong. Diagnose
  before continuing.
- **Day 45**: lead→close rate < 3%. Either inbound flow is broken
  or you&apos;re not following up. Fix before adding volume.
- **Day 60**: zero LinkedIn DM replies. Either profile / message /
  targeting is wrong. Use doc 21 troubleshooting.
- **Day 75**: zero blog traffic. Check Google Search Console for
  indexing issues. Submit sitemap again. Check for crawler blocks.
- **Day 90**: total revenue from new effort < $500. The plan
  isn&apos;t working OR you haven&apos;t executed it. Honestly
  assess which.
