# SMS & Voicemail Scripts

**Critical compliance points:**

- **TCPA / CTIA:** Do not text strangers from your business line without prior consent or a clear B2B context. Cold-texting a phone number you didn't legitimately obtain is illegal and gets your number flagged. Only text:
  1. People who handed you their card / wrote their number on a form
  2. Existing customers (you have consent in your TOS)
  3. Numbers publicly listed as a *business* line where the business invites contact
- Always include "Reply STOP to opt out" on the first message of any sequence.
- Sign every SMS with "— NOHO Mailbox".
- Keep messages under 160 characters when possible to avoid MMS conversion charges.

## Phone-in pickup script (when someone calls to book)

```
1. "NOHO Mailbox, this is [Your name]."
2. "Sure thing — pickup address?"
3. "Drop-off?"
4. "Got it. That's [zone — pull from rate sheet]. $[X]. Earliest pickup is [time]."
5. "Card on file or pay on pickup?"
6. "[Confirm both addresses out loud]. We'll text you when the driver is rolling."
```

**Always close with:** "Anything else? Stamps, notary, packaging?" → cross-sell hook.

## Walk-in pitch (30 seconds)

When someone walks into a target business cold:

> "Hi — I'm [Your name] from NOHO Mailbox, the storefront on Lankershim by [landmark]. We do five-dollar same-day delivery inside North Hollywood. No membership, no minimum. Most [law firms / florists / print shops] don't even know we exist — first run's on us if you want to try it. Card?"

→ hand a business card → "If you want, I can put a sticker on your door so your team has the number when they need a run."

That last line is high-conversion: physical door sticker = constant reminder.
