# Annual Planning Template

The 4-hour annual planning session that sets the year. Run this in
late December for the next calendar year, or end of fiscal Q4 if
you&apos;re fiscal-year aligned.

**Format**: 4-hour solo session OR full-day with co-founders. Output:
3-page memo. Save in `/sales-kit/_annual-plans/[YEAR].md`.

---

## Pre-session prep (week before)

Pull these data:

1. **Last year&apos;s revenue** by month. Identify trend (growing /
   flat / declining).
2. **Last year&apos;s customer count** by product line. Same trend.
3. **Last year&apos;s churn rate**. By product line.
4. **Last year&apos;s top 5 lead sources** (by closed customers).
5. **Last year&apos;s biggest wins** (3 specific things).
6. **Last year&apos;s biggest mistakes** (3 specific things).
7. **Last year&apos;s strategic plan** — what was the goal? What
   actually happened?

This data prevents wishful planning.

---

## Section 1: Reality check (45 min)

### Questions to answer

1. **Revenue last year (actual)**: $___
2. **Revenue forecast this year if we change nothing**: $___
3. **Profit margin last year**: ___%
4. **Cash on hand**: $___
5. **Months of runway at current burn**: ___
6. **Top 3 customer types** (by revenue contribution): ____
7. **Concentration risk** (does any single customer = >10% of
   revenue?): ____
8. **Competitive position**: Are we gaining share, holding, or losing
   to specific competitors?

### Honest filter

If any of these are flashing red:
- Cash runway < 6 months
- Customer concentration > 30% in one customer
- Margin shrinking 2+ quarters in a row
- Top competitor growing 50%+ faster than us

The annual plan should DIRECTLY address them. Don&apos;t plan around
problems — plan to fix them.

---

## Section 2: The five-engine review (45 min)

For each engine, score 1-10 on three dimensions:

### 1. Mailbox plans (storefront)

- Growth: how much did this engine grow last year? (1-10)
- Profitability: contribution margin this engine? (1-10)
- Strategic value: does this engine compound or decay? (1-10)

### 2. Same-day delivery

- Growth: ___ / 10
- Profitability: ___ / 10
- Strategic value: ___ / 10

### 3. Business Solutions ($2k bundle)

- Growth: ___ / 10
- Profitability: ___ / 10
- Strategic value: ___ / 10

### 4. CMRA SaaS

- Growth: ___ / 10
- Profitability: ___ / 10
- Strategic value: ___ / 10

### 5. Affiliate + Partner programs

- Growth: ___ / 10
- Profitability: ___ / 10
- Strategic value: ___ / 10

### Decision rules

- **Score 7+ on all 3**: invest more.
- **Score 5-6 average**: maintain, don&apos;t invest more.
- **Score 4 or below on any**: prune or radically rethink.

You can&apos;t scale 5 engines simultaneously with limited resources.
Pick the 1-2 that score highest and deserve doubling-down.

---

## Section 3: Strategic priorities (60 min)

### One year from today, what does the business look like?

Write 3-5 bullets describing the state of the business 12 months from
now. Be specific:

- Revenue: $___
- Customers: ___
- Team size: ___
- Geographic footprint: ___
- Product line changes: ___

### What 3-5 things have to happen for that future to be real?

These are your strategic priorities. They should be:
- Few enough to focus on (3-5 max)
- Specific enough to plan around (not "grow revenue")
- Measurable so you know if you hit them
- Time-bound (Q1 / Q2 / Q3 / Q4 milestones)

Examples of good priorities:
- "Hit $50k MRR on storefront mailbox plans by Q3"
- "Onboard 10 CMRA SaaS operators by EOY"
- "Launch and prove a new revenue lane: [specific lane] by Q2"

Examples of bad priorities (vague, unmeasurable):
- "Grow revenue"
- "Become a market leader"
- "Improve customer experience"

### What are the 3-5 things you SHOULDN&apos;T do this year?

The hardest part of strategy is what you say no to. List:
- Product lines you won&apos;t expand into
- Customer segments you won&apos;t chase
- Markets you won&apos;t enter
- Features you won&apos;t build
- Partnerships you won&apos;t pursue

This list keeps you focused mid-year when shiny objects appear.

---

## Section 4: Quarterly milestones (60 min)

Map each strategic priority to quarterly milestones.

### Template

```
Priority: [Priority 1]

Q1 milestone: [Specific deliverable + date]
Q2 milestone: [Specific deliverable + date]
Q3 milestone: [Specific deliverable + date]
Q4 milestone: [Specific deliverable + date]

What could go wrong: [Top 2-3 risks]
How we&apos;d know: [Leading indicators that the priority is on / off
track]
```

Repeat for each strategic priority.

---

## Section 5: Revenue model + budget (30 min)

### Revenue model (3-line forecast)

- **Conservative**: assume 10-20% growth from current trend
- **Base**: assume 30-50% growth (plan you&apos;re actively executing)
- **Stretch**: assume 80-150% growth (everything goes right)

### Budget

For each revenue scenario, what&apos;s the budget?

- People: salaries / contractors
- Software / hosting / tools
- Marketing: paid ads, content, events
- Office: rent, utilities, supplies
- Inventory / packaging (if applicable)
- Reserve / cash buffer

### Decision: which scenario do we plan for?

Most companies plan for "Base" and budget for "Conservative." That
way you have buffer if things go slower than expected.

---

## Section 6: People plan (30 min)

Honest questions:

1. **Are you / your team capacity-constrained?** (Yes if you&apos;re
   working > 50 hours/week consistently and still can&apos;t finish
   priorities.)
2. **What roles do you NEED to hire this year?**
3. **What roles do you WANT but can defer?**
4. **What roles do you currently have that aren&apos;t pulling
   weight?** (This is the hard question.)

Rule of thumb: don&apos;t hire until pain is acute. But don&apos;t
wait until burnout either. Hire when:
- A specific function is bottlenecking growth
- You&apos;ve documented the role / playbook (so the new hire can
  succeed)
- You can afford 12 months of salary at the conservative-revenue
  scenario

---

## Section 7: Risks + contingencies (15 min)

What could go wrong this year?

External:
- Economic recession
- Major competitor entering / exiting market
- Regulatory change (USPS rules, CMRA rules, tax law)
- Geographic event (LA earthquake, fire, etc.)

Internal:
- Co-founder departure
- Key staff departure
- Cash crunch
- Major customer churn
- Operational failure (audit failure, security breach)

For top 3 risks, what&apos;s the contingency plan? Don&apos;t need
detailed planning — just identify what you&apos;d do.

---

## Section 8: Team kickoff (60 min — only if you have a team)

If you have employees / co-founders / regular contractors:

1. **Share the plan** (the 3-page memo)
2. **Assign owners** to each strategic priority
3. **Set the cadence** (weekly check-ins, monthly reviews, quarterly
   resets)
4. **Define success** for each owner
5. **Define what they&apos;re NOT responsible for** (clear scope =
   reduces stress + politics)

Without a team: skip this section. Solo founders can self-execute the
plan, but writing it down + reviewing weekly still matters.

---

## Quarterly reset (every 90 days)

Each quarter, take 30 minutes to:

1. Review the 3 strategic priorities
2. Are quarterly milestones being hit? Why / why not?
3. Has the world changed enough to revise the plan?
4. Adjust + recommit

If you&apos;re consistently missing milestones for 6+ months, the
plan is wrong — not the execution. Rewrite.

---

## What this template ISN&apos;T

- **Not a financial model.** Use a real spreadsheet for forecasting.
- **Not a substitute for talking to customers.** Plan based on what
  customers actually want, not what you think they want.
- **Not for crisis mode.** If runway < 3 months, this is the wrong
  tool. Crisis mode = "what do I cut today?" — different document.

---

## Output format (the 3-page memo)

### Page 1: Where we are
- Last year&apos;s revenue + 3-bullet summary
- Last year&apos;s big wins (top 3)
- Last year&apos;s big mistakes (top 3)
- Current state of each engine (1-line each)

### Page 2: Where we&apos;re going
- 1-year vision (3-5 bullets)
- 3-5 strategic priorities (with quarterly milestones)
- 3-5 things we won&apos;t do
- Revenue scenarios (conservative / base / stretch)

### Page 3: How we get there
- People plan (hires this year)
- Budget summary
- Top 3 risks + contingencies
- Quarterly review cadence

Save to `/sales-kit/_annual-plans/[YEAR].md`.

---

## When to NOT do annual planning

Annual planning works when:
- You have 6+ months of consistent business operations
- You have stable revenue you can forecast against
- You have multiple revenue lanes worth optimizing across
- You have decision-making authority over the priorities

It doesn&apos;t work when:
- You&apos;re < 6 months old (you don&apos;t have enough data)
- You&apos;re in crisis (focus on this week, not this year)
- You&apos;re fundamentally re-platforming or pivoting (plan the
  pivot, not the year)
- The CEO doesn&apos;t commit (annual plan only matters if it&apos;s
  the CEO&apos;s plan)

For early-stage / pre-PMF businesses, quarterly planning at most.
