## Script 6 — "LLC for rental property — do you need one?" (60s)

**Hook (0-3s)**: [On screen: "Single-property LLC?"] / "You bought a
rental. Should you put it in an LLC?"

**Body (3-55s)**:
"Three reasons to use an LLC for a rental:

One: liability separation. If a tenant sues over a slip-and-fall, they
can come after the LLC's assets — that one property — but not your
personal house, car, savings.

Two: privacy. The LLC's address goes on county records, not yours. So
if a disgruntled tenant goes looking, they find a P.O. address, not
where you live.

Three: tax treatment. Pass-through taxation, but with the option to
elect S-corp status if rental income gets significant.

Two reasons NOT to use one:

If your state charges high franchise tax — California's $800/year per
LLC adds up fast at 5+ properties.

If you're underwater on the property — equity stripping doesn't work
if there's no equity.

Standard playbook for 5+ properties: layered LLCs. Each property in its
own LLC, all owned by a Wyoming or NM holding company. That's anonymity
plus protection plus efficiency."

## Production notes

**Length**:
- TikTok / Reels: 30-60 seconds (Script 2, 5, 7 ideal)
- YouTube Shorts: up to 60 seconds (most scripts above)
- YouTube long-form (re-edit): combine 3-5 scripts into a 7-minute
  primer ("LLC for solopreneurs: everything you need to know")

**B-roll suggestions**:
- Storefront exterior (5062 Lankershim)
- Sorting mail / package handling
- Computer screens showing admin dashboard
- Form 1583 being notarized
- Driver loading van with same-day deliveries

**Subtitles required**: Yes for all formats. Most short-form is watched
muted. Use TikTok's auto-caption + manual review for accuracy.

**Posting cadence**:
- TikTok: 1 short / day
- IG Reels: 3-4 / week
- YouTube Shorts: 2-3 / week
- YouTube long-form: 1 video every 2-3 weeks

**CTA consistency**: Always end with "link in bio" or "link in the
comments." Don't try to fit URLs on-screen — they're hard to type while
watching short-form.

**Affiliate adaptation**: Affiliates rewrite the CTA with their
tracking link: "use my link, [their_url]?aff=THEIR_CODE for the same
deal."
