# Hiring Roadmap — When to Hire Each Role

A staged hiring plan from solo operator to $5M/mo organization. Each
role tied to a revenue trigger, NOT a desire.

**Principle**: Hire when pain is acute and a clear playbook exists.
Don&apos;t hire to feel less alone. Don&apos;t hire to delegate work
you haven&apos;t systematized.

---

## Stage 1: Solo founder (0 — $30k MRR)

You are: founder, salesperson, customer support, content writer,
operations, and software developer.

**Don&apos;t hire anyone yet.** Use the sales kit playbooks. Spend
30 min/day on outbound (doc 22), 30 min/day on inbound triage (doc
31), 30 min/day on content (doc 30).

Tools that replace hires:
- Gusto / OnPay for payroll (when LLC + S-corp election kicks in)
- Stripe / Mercury for banking
- Cancellation: nothing in this stage requires headcount

**Exit trigger**: $30k MRR consistent for 3 months.

---

## Stage 2: First hire — Storefront associate ($30k — $50k MRR)

**Why this one first**: The walk-in storefront is the bottleneck for
the founder. While the founder is sorting mail, they&apos;re not
selling, not making content, not closing $2k bundles.

**What they do**:
- Mail intake + scanning (the 9am-2pm core hours)
- New customer onboarding at the counter
- Basic phone support
- Light same-day delivery dispatch coordination

**Expected hire**:
- $18-22/hr W-2 (CA)
- 30-40 hours/week
- $30-40k/yr total comp + payroll tax

**Trigger to hire**: when you (the founder) are:
- Spending 4+ hours/day on counter work
- Missing inbound calls 3+ times/week
- Falling behind on content / outbound (the revenue-generating
  activities)

**Skills**: Reliable, customer-facing, can learn POS systems and
basic scanning. NOT a sales rep — just operations.

---

## Stage 3: Second hire — Driver / fulfillment ($40k — $80k MRR)

**Why second**: same-day delivery growth blocked by driver capacity.
Driver hires unlock revenue, not just relieve work.

**What they do**:
- Same-day delivery routes (3-7 runs/day starting)
- Recurring B2B routes (5-9 runs/week per account)
- Vehicle maintenance + receipts
- Customer hand-off photos / signatures

**Expected hire**:
- $20-25/hr W-2 OR $30/hour 1099 contractor
- 30-40 hours/week
- $35-50k/yr total comp + insurance + vehicle if you provide

**Trigger to hire**: when you have:
- 5+ recurring same-day customers asking for daily routes
- Customer requests on hold because driver capacity is maxed
- Founder personally driving more than 2 routes/week

**Skills**: Reliable driver, clean DMV record, comfortable with B2B
customers (not retail-driver attitude), ideally with a personal van /
truck (cuts your vehicle cost).

---

## Stage 4: Third hire — Customer success / sales ($60k — $120k MRR)

**Why third**: At ~50+ active customers + growing inbound, response
time slips. Founder can&apos;t personally first-touch every Tier B
lead. Need someone to close the easy stuff.

**What they do**:
- First-touch on tier B inbound (mailbox / delivery quotes)
- Customer success calls (existing customer renewals + upgrades)
- Phone overflow during business hours
- $2k bundle inquiries (initial qualifying call, then escalate to
  founder for close)

**Expected hire**:
- $50-70k base + 5-15% commission on closes
- $70-100k OTE

**Trigger to hire**: when you have:
- 30+ inbound leads/week
- Lead→close rate dropping (because follow-up is slipping)
- Founder spending 4+ hours/day on inbound — at the cost of strategic
  work

**Skills**: B2B inside-sales experience preferred. Comfortable on
phone + email. Not a marketer — a closer.

---

## Stage 5: Fourth hire — Marketing / content ($80k — $200k MRR)

**Why fourth**: Content + paid ads + SEO maintenance becomes its own
job at this scale. Founder shouldn&apos;t be writing every blog post
and managing every ad campaign.

**What they do**:
- Execute the 90-day content calendar (doc 30)
- Manage paid ads (doc 19) — bidding, optimization, creative
- Manage social channels (LinkedIn, IG, TikTok, YouTube)
- Coordinate affiliate program operations
- Email newsletter writing + sending

**Expected hire**:
- $60-80k base
- Senior version: $80-120k base

**Trigger to hire**: when you&apos;re:
- Consistently skipping content calendar items (doc 22 priority order
  showing skipped items every week)
- Paid ads sub-optimized (because no one&apos;s tweaking bids)
- Affiliate program inquiries piling up unanswered

**Skills**: Demonstrable B2B / SaaS marketing track record. Specific
expertise in 1-2 areas (SEO, paid ads, content) — not a generalist.

---

## Stage 6: Fifth hire — Operations manager ($150k — $300k MRR)

**Why fifth**: At 100+ active customers + 5+ employees, daily
operations need a manager who isn&apos;t the founder.

**What they do**:
- Schedule + coordinate storefront staff
- Manage USPS-CMRA compliance + reporting
- Vendor management (UPS / FedEx / printer / supplies)
- Customer complaint resolution (escalations from CS)
- Operations metrics (mail volume, scanning turnaround, etc.)

**Expected hire**:
- $70-95k

**Trigger to hire**: when:
- Compliance backlog forming (1583s missing, USPS reports late)
- Staff scheduling becoming a headache
- Founder spending more than 1 day/week on operations

**Skills**: Operations management background (3-5+ years). Comfortable
with regulatory compliance. Not customer-facing primarily.

---

## Stage 7: Sixth hire — SDR / outbound seller ($200k — $500k MRR)

**Why sixth**: CMRA SaaS engine needs dedicated outbound. The LinkedIn
outbound + cold email sequences (docs 21, 28) work but require 1-2
hours/day per active sales rep. Founder/closer pair structure stops
working at this scale.

**What they do**:
- LinkedIn outbound (50+ connection requests + 25+ DMs per day)
- Cold email outbound (sequences A/B/C from doc 28)
- Demo booking
- Hand off qualified demos to founder/account executive for close

**Expected hire**:
- $50-65k base + commission ($15-25k OTE for 3-5 closes/quarter)
- $80-100k total OTE

**Trigger to hire**: when:
- LinkedIn connection requests / cold email volume is below target
  (because it&apos;s being deprioritized)
- SaaS demo bookings dropping (top-of-funnel problem)
- Founder spending 1+ day/week on SaaS outbound

**Skills**: SDR experience (1-3 years). Comfortable with cold
outreach. Coachable — playbook-driven role.

---

## Stage 8: Account executive ($300k — $1M MRR)

**Why eighth**: Closing CMRA SaaS deals consistently requires
follow-up + relationship building over weeks. Founder shouldn&apos;t
be the only closer at this scale.

**What they do**:
- 30-min product demos (using doc 29 demo walkthrough script)
- Migration scoping calls
- Negotiation + close
- First-30-days customer onboarding (handed off after close)

**Expected hire**:
- $80-120k base + 5-10% commission on closed ARR
- $130-200k total OTE for solid AE

**Trigger to hire**: when:
- 4+ qualified demos/week and the founder can&apos;t do them all
- Time-to-close stretching from 3-4 weeks to 8-12 weeks (because
  follow-up is slow)
- Founder wants to step back from individual deals to focus on
  strategy

**Skills**: B2B SaaS AE experience (3-5 years). Demonstrable closes
in a comparable price range ($300-$1500/mo SaaS). Excellent at email
+ phone.

---

## Stage 9: Engineering hire ($500k — $1.5M MRR)

**Why ninth**: SaaS product needs ongoing development. Founder
can&apos;t be the only one shipping code at scale.

**What they do**:
- Product engineering (new features, bug fixes)
- Customer-driven prioritization
- Infrastructure / hosting
- Support engineering for B2B customers

**Expected hire**:
- $130-180k full-time senior engineer (CA market)
- OR: $80-150/hr contractor (3-month engagements)

**Trigger to hire**: when:
- Product feature backlog growing faster than founder can ship
- B2B customers asking for features that need 2+ weeks of dev work
- Bug response time slipping (4-12hr SLA breaking)

**Skills**: TypeScript / React / Next.js / Postgres / Prisma stack.
B2B SaaS experience preferred. Not an architecture astronaut — needs
to ship.

---

## Stage 10: General manager ($1M — $3M MRR)

**Why tenth**: Founder transitions from doing-everything to
leading-the-org.

**What they do**:
- Manage all functional leads (CS, sales, ops, marketing)
- Hold the org accountable to plans (doc 34)
- Hire next round of leadership
- Remove blockers for founder to focus on strategic moves

**Expected hire**:
- $150-220k base + equity / bonus

**Trigger to hire**: when:
- Founder is spending more than 30% of time on people management
- Hiring decisions need to keep happening without founder bandwidth
- Multiple functional teams (CS + sales + ops + marketing) need
  cross-coordination

**Skills**: General-management background (8+ years). Functional
leadership experience in 1-2 areas. Has scaled an org from
$1M-$10M before.

## What NOT to hire (or hire late)

These roles often get hired too early:

- **Office manager**: until you&apos;re 8+ employees in office, the
  GM or operations manager handles this.
- **Recruiter**: until you&apos;re hiring 5+/year, founder/GM
  handles.
- **Executive assistant**: until founder is consistently working >
  60 hours/week, this is a solve-by-doing-less issue.
- **CFO**: until you&apos;re actively raising capital or doing
  complex tax structures, your CPA + bookkeeper is enough.
- **PR / Communications**: until you&apos;re newsworthy, this is the
  founder + the press release templates (doc 35).
- **Product manager**: until you have 2+ engineers, the founder is
  the PM.
- **Designer (in-house)**: until brand work is constant, contract
  designers are cheaper.

## Hiring playbook (every hire)

For each role:

### Before posting
1. **Document the role**: what they do, what they don&apos;t, what
   success looks like at 30/60/90 days
2. **Document the playbook**: how they&apos;ll do the job (link to
   relevant sales-kit docs)
3. **Set the comp range** and confirm cash runway covers 12 months
4. **Define the interview process** (who, what, decision criteria)

### During hiring
5. **Source candidates**: LinkedIn referrals, network referrals,
   then job boards (Indeed, AngelList, We Work Remotely)
6. **Phone screen** (30 min) — fit check
7. **Technical / skills interview** (60-90 min) — can they do the
   work?
8. **Cultural / values interview** (60 min) — can we work together?
9. **Reference checks** (3 references, 15-20 min each) — non-negotiable
10. **Offer** with clear comp + equity + benefits

### After hire
11. **30-day onboarding plan**: what they should know by day 30
12. **30/60/90 check-ins**: are they on track?
13. **First 90 days are evaluative both ways**: easy out for either
    side if not working

---

## When to fire

If after 90 days a hire isn&apos;t pulling weight:

1. **Make the gap explicit**. "Here&apos;s what I expected, here&apos;s
   what I&apos;m seeing, here&apos;s the gap."
2. **30-day improvement plan with specific milestones**.
3. **Weekly check-ins on the milestones**.
4. **At end of 30 days**: clear yes/no decision. No "let&apos;s give
   it another month."

Most underperformers hired into early-stage roles don&apos;t recover.
The best move is fast separation. The cost of a bad hire is 3-5x
their salary in lost momentum + missed work. Don&apos;t let it
linger.

## $5M/mo organization rough headcount

If we hit the $5M MRR target:

| Function | Headcount | Total comp |
|---|---|---|
| Founder + leadership | 2-3 | $400-600k |
| Sales (AE + SDR) | 4-6 | $600k-1M |
| Customer success | 3-4 | $300-450k |
| Operations (storefront + dispatch + compliance) | 6-10 | $400-700k |
| Engineering | 3-5 | $500-900k |
| Marketing / content | 2-3 | $200-360k |
| GM / ops manager | 1 | $200k |
| **Total** | **21-32** | **$2.6-4.2M / yr** |

Total payroll = ~$2.6-4.2M annually. At $5M MRR = $60M ARR, that&apos;s
4-7% of revenue — sustainable for a software-services business.

This isn&apos;t the only way to get to $5M. Could be done with fewer
people if SaaS engine is more dominant. Could be done with more if
storefront / delivery scales heavily.

---

## What this template ISN&apos;T

- Not a guarantee. Hiring is hard; some hires will fail.
- Not a substitute for talking to candidates. Read this, then do the
  actual hiring well.
- Not relevant if you&apos;re bootstrapping at <$30k MRR. Stay solo.
- Not for VC-backed companies — they hire faster (and often blow up
  faster). This template is for sustainable bootstrap growth.
